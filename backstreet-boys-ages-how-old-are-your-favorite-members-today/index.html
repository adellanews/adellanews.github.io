The Backstreet Boys: A Brief History

The story of the Backstreet Boys is one of incredible success and persistence. They got their start in Orlando, Florida, way back in 1993. Their aim was to bring back the harmony-rich sounds of vocal groups from earlier times, but with a fresh, pop twist. You know, it was a different time for music then.

Formation and Early Success

The group came together through auditions and connections within the music scene. Kevin Richardson was already friends with Brian Littrell, his cousin. Nick Carter, Howie Dorough, and AJ McLean were found through other means. They quickly started to get a following in Europe first, which is interesting, before making it big in their home country. Their very first album, released internationally, showed off their blend of catchy tunes and smooth voices. It was pretty clear, right from the start, that they had something special.

A Sensation Around the World

By the late 1990s, the Backstreet Boys were a true sensation around the world. Their albums, like "Backstreet's Back" and "Millennium," sold millions of copies. They played to packed arenas and their songs were everywhere. It's almost hard to imagine how popular they were, with fans screaming for them wherever they went. They really became a household name, more or less, and their music became the soundtrack for so many people's younger years.

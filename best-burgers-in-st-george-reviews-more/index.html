Best Burgers in St. George: Reviews & More

Emily Cortez
• Jan 05, 2026 environment and climate

Is St. George, Utah, poised to become a culinary hotspot, eclipsing even the vibrant food scene of Las Vegas? The bold claim is being whispered, fueled by a surge of new restaurant openings and a buzz of excitement amongst residents. From fast-casual favorites to gourmet burger joints, the southwestern Utah city is rapidly transforming its dining landscape.

The recent 2024 State of the City address ignited a wave of anticipation, with the announcement of several new dining establishments slated to open in the Southern Parkway area, near Desert Color and the St. George Regional Airport. This development comes nearly 17 years after the arrival of the first In-N-Out Burger in Washington, Utah, an event that sparked unprecedented queues and solidified the region's appetite for quality fast food. Now, with the promise of even more diverse options, St. George is staking its claim as a serious contender in the culinary arena.

City: St. George, Utah
State: Utah
Key Development: Multiple new restaurants announced in 2024 State of the City address, focused on the Southern Parkway area.
Notable Restaurant Openings: In-N-Out Burger (historical reference, 2007, Washington, UT), plus anticipated future openings.
Local Favorites: Wayback Burgers, Five Guys Burgers and Fries.
Reference: https://www.stgeorgeutah.com/

The clamor for In-N-Out Burger back in 2007, with lines stretching for hours, even months after its grand opening, speaks volumes about the region's pent-up demand for iconic food experiences. The forthcoming In-N-Out location, planned for Exit 2 on the Southern Parkway, near Terrible's gas station and across from Big Shots Golf, is expected to generate similar enthusiasm. The proposed 3,887 square foot restaurant, featuring both indoor and outdoor seating, is designed to accommodate the anticipated crowds.

But In-N-Out is just one piece of the puzzle. The broader restaurant scene in St. George is blossoming, offering everything from classic American fare at Wayback Burgers, located at 2134 East Riverside Drive, to the customizable creations at Five Guys Burgers and Fries on East 100 South. With over 250,000 burger combinations and more than 1,000 milkshake options at Five Guys alone, diners are spoiled for choice.

Online reviews and testimonials paint a vivid picture of the burgeoning culinary landscape. Visitors rave about the speed and efficiency of service, with some cashiers reportedly taking orders in under 10 seconds. The welcoming atmosphere, even for those with accents, underscores the friendly and approachable nature of the local dining scene. With thousands of reviews and hundreds of photos circulating online, the buzz surrounding St. Georges restaurants is undeniable.

The planned developments along the Southern Parkway promise to further elevate the dining experience in St. George. This strategic location, easily accessible from various parts of the city and surrounding areas, is poised to become a dining destination. With a blend of established chains and potentially new, independent ventures, the area is set to cater to a diverse range of tastes and preferences.

Beyond burgers, the city boasts a wider array of culinary delights. TripAdvisor reveals nearly 10,000 reviews of burger joints alone, with options ranging in price and location. This depth and breadth of options demonstrate St. George's commitment to providing a satisfying dining experience for all. Whether youre a local resident or a visitor passing through on your way to Zion National Park, the question remains: are you ready to experience the evolving flavors of St. George?

The transformation of St. George's culinary scene is a testament to the citys growth and vibrancy. The combination of established favorites and exciting new additions is creating a dining landscape that rivals larger metropolitan areas. While the comparison to Las Vegas might be a bold one, the enthusiasm and momentum behind St. Georges restaurant scene are undeniable. Only time will tell if it can truly surpass the entertainment capital, but one thing is certain: St. George is hungry for success.

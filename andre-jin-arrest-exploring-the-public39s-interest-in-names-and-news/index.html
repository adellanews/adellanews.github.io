The name André, as our text points out, has quite an old background. It comes from the ancient Greek "andréas (ἀνδρέας)." This Greek term, in turn, gets its meaning from "anḗr ‎ (ἀνήρ) andrós ‎ (ἀνδρός)," which means "man, adult male, husband." So, essentially, the name André means "manly." It’s a very strong and traditional meaning, which is pretty interesting when you think about it.

It's also worth noting that André is a male given name that has been borrowed from the French or Portuguese forms of Andrew.

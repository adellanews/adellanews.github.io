How the French First Lady Role Is Funded

Unlike some other countries, the role of the French First Lady is not a formally paid position. Brigitte Macron does not receive a salary from the state for her duties. This is a key point when considering her "worth" in financial terms, because, you know, it means her public role doesn't directly add to her personal income.

However, the First Lady does have a budget allocated to her office to cover staff, administrative costs, and expenses related to her public engagements. This budget supports her activities, such as representing France at events, supporting charitable causes, and managing her schedule. It's a practical arrangement, really, to allow her to perform her duties effectively.

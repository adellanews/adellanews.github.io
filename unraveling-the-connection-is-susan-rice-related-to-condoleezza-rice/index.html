Who is Susan Rice?

Susan Rice is a seasoned diplomat and policy advisor who has played a pivotal role in shaping U.S. foreign policy. Born on November 17, 1964, in Washington, D.C., she grew up in a family that valued education and civic engagement. Rice attended Stanford University, where she earned her bachelor’s degree in international relations. She later pursued her Ph.D. at Oxford University as a Rhodes Scholar. Her career includes significant positions such as the U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations and National Security Advisor under President Barack Obama.

Who is Condoleezza Rice?

Condoleezza Rice, born on November 14, 1954, in Birmingham, Alabama, is another influential figure in American politics. She grew up during the civil rights movement and was inspired by the struggles of her community. Rice attended the University of Denver and later earned her Ph.D. in political science from the University of Notre Dame. Her career highlights include serving as National Security Advisor and Secretary of State under President George W. Bush, making her the first African American woman to hold that position.

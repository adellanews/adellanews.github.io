The Celestial Wonder: August's Sturgeon Moon

What Makes It Special

Beyond the world of fictional bands, the term "August moon" also points to a real and beautiful event in our night sky: the full moon that appears in August. This moon has a special name, the Sturgeon Moon. It's named after the giant freshwater fish that were often caught in abundance during this time of year by Native American tribes, particularly in the Great Lakes and Lake Champlain areas, so. This connection to nature gives the August moon a deep, historical meaning, isn't that interesting?

The Sturgeon Moon will peak on Monday, August 19, offering a chance for people to look up and see its full illumination.

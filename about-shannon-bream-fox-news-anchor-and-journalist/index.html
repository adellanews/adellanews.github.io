About Shannon Bream: Fox News Anchor and Journalist

Emily Beck
• Jan 05, 2026 science and technology

What does it take to transition from a beauty pageant winner to a respected legal analyst and anchor of a major news network's flagship Sunday program? Shannon Bream's journey is a testament to the power of dedication, resilience, and a relentless pursuit of knowledge. From her early days in Florida to the hallowed halls of the Supreme Court, Bream's story is one of evolving ambitions and unwavering faith.

Born Shannon Noelle DePuy on December 23, 1970, in Sanford, Florida, Bream's path to journalism wasn't a straight line. Her parents, Marie Norris and Ed DePuy, provided a solid foundation, instilling in her the values that would shape her career. She excelled academically at North Florida Christian High School in Tallahassee, setting the stage for her higher education pursuits at Liberty University in Lynchburg, Virginia. There, she earned a business degree in 1993, a seemingly conventional start that belied the extraordinary trajectory her life would soon take.

Following her graduation from Liberty University, Bream felt a pull toward law. She enrolled at Florida State University College of Law, immersing herself in legal studies and ultimately earning her Juris Doctor degree. During this time, she also tapped into her poise and presence, competing in and winning the Miss Florida USA pageant in 1995. This experience, while seemingly disparate from her legal aspirations, honed her public speaking skills and provided a glimpse into the world of media and communication.

After law school, Bream embarked on a legal career in Tampa, Florida, specializing in corporate law. However, the allure of journalism persisted, a quiet whisper that eventually grew into a resounding call. She made the bold decision to pivot, trading the courtroom for the newsroom, a transition that would prove to be both challenging and ultimately rewarding.

Bream's early years in television news saw her working as a local news reporter and anchor. This foundational experience provided invaluable training in the fast-paced world of broadcast journalism. She honed her skills in reporting, interviewing, and delivering news with clarity and precision. Her talent and dedication didnt go unnoticed, and in 2007, she joined Fox News Channel.

At Fox News, Bream quickly rose through the ranks. She served as a Supreme Court correspondent, covering landmark cases and legal developments with insightful analysis. Her legal background provided a unique perspective, allowing her to dissect complex legal issues with clarity and authority. She then anchored "Fox News @ Night" for five years, demonstrating her versatility and command of live television. In 2022, she achieved a career milestone, becoming the anchor of "Fox News Sunday," the network's prestigious Sunday morning political talk show. This appointment marked her as a leading voice in political journalism.

Beyond her on-air presence, Bream is a prolific author. Her books, including "The Women of the Bible Speak" and "Finding the Bright Side," delve into themes of faith, resilience, and finding purpose in challenging times. These works reflect her deep personal faith and her commitment to sharing inspiring stories with a wider audience.

Bream's journey hasn't been without its challenges. She has openly discussed her struggles with chronic pain, a health issue that has tested her resilience and strengthened her resolve. Her transparency about her personal struggles has resonated with many, demonstrating her vulnerability and humanizing her public persona. She and her husband, Sheldon Bream, whom she married in 1995, have also navigated their own set of difficulties, strengthening their bond through shared experiences.

Shannon Bream's story is a compelling narrative of a woman who dared to pursue her passions, embrace change, and overcome adversity. From the courtroom to the newsroom, she has blazed a trail, proving that with determination and unwavering faith, anything is possible. Her voice resonates with authority and compassion, making her a prominent figure in American journalism and an inspiration to those who aspire to follow their own unique paths.

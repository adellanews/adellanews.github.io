Understanding the Name Alyssa

The name "Alyssa" is, in some respects, quite charming, and it has a history that stretches back quite a bit. You know, it’s interesting to think about how a name can carry such lovely connotations.

Apparently, the way "Alyssa" is spelled today might even be influenced by the "alyssum" flower. This flower's name, you see, comes from a Greek word, which is pretty cool. The Greek word, "ἀ (a)," acts as a negative prefix, combined with "λύσσα," which means "rage" or "madness." So, "alyssum" literally means "without madness" or "calm," which is often why the flower was historically thought to calm excitement. It's a bit of a linguistic journey, isn't it, to see how a name's spelling might pick up cues from the natural world?

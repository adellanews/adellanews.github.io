What is Down Syndrome?

Down syndrome is a genetic disorder caused by the presence of an extra chromosome 21, leading to developmental and intellectual challenges. Individuals with Down syndrome may exhibit distinct physical characteristics, including a flat facial profile, slanted eyes, and a protruding tongue, among others. However, it is important to recognize that each person with Down syndrome is unique, and their abilities and personalities vary widely.

What Are Some Common Myths About Down Syndrome?

Despite increased awareness, several myths persist regarding Down syndrome. Understanding and dispelling these misconceptions is essential for fostering acceptance. Here are some common myths:

  • Myth 1: All individuals with Down syndrome have the same abilities.
  • Myth 2: Down syndrome is a disease that can be cured.
  • Myth 3: Individuals with Down syndrome cannot lead independent lives.
  • Myth 4: Down syndrome is caused by a lack of proper care during pregnancy.

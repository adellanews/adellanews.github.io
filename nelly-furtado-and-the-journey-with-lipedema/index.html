What is Lipedema?

Lipedema is a chronic condition characterized by an abnormal accumulation of fat cells, primarily in the legs, thighs, buttocks, and sometimes the arms. This condition is often confused with general obesity, but it is a distinct disorder that can lead to physical discomfort and emotional distress. Individuals with lipedema may experience symptoms such as:

  • Swelling and heaviness in the limbs
  • Easy bruising
  • Pain and tenderness in affected areas
  • Skin changes, such as a dimpled appearance

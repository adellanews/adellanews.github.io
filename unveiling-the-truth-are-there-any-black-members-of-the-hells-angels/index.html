What is the History of the Hells Angels?

The Hells Angels Motorcycle Club was founded in 1948 in California and quickly grew into one of the most infamous motorcycle clubs in the world. Originally formed by a group of veterans returning from World War II, the club's mission was to promote camaraderie among motorcycle enthusiasts. Over the years, the Hells Angels expanded across the United States and internationally, becoming synonymous with motorcycle culture.

Do Hells Angels Have a Code of Conduct?

Yes, the Hells Angels are known for their strict code of conduct, which emphasizes loyalty, respect, and brotherhood among members. This code governs the behavior and actions of all members and is considered sacred. Violating this code can result in severe consequences, including expulsion from the club. The emphasis on loyalty often overshadows discussions about diversity and inclusion within the group.

Charlotte Adams
• Jan 12, 2026 environment and climate

Is Halloween truly the sweetest holiday of the year? The National Confectioners Association (NCA) reports a staggering $4.6 billion in candy sales annually for Halloween, with chocolate alone accounting for a massive $3.1 billion.

The allure of Halloween, with its costumes, spooky decorations, and, of course, the mountains of candy, continues to captivate people of all ages. Data from Statista reveals that approximately 70% of Americans participate in the holiday in some way, shape, or form. With over 70 million children under the age of 18 eagerly anticipating their trick-or-treat hauls, the demand for sweets is undeniably high. The holiday has evolved, too, becoming an opportunity for creativity in treats, gifts, and themed gatherings. Candy isn't just a simple treat; it is an essential aspect of Halloween, contributing significantly to both the holiday's economic impact and its festive spirit.

Heres a breakdown of the top candy categories sold during Halloween, providing a clearer picture of which sweets reign supreme during this candy-centric celebration. This information offers valuable insights into consumer preferences and the trends shaping the confectionery industry during the most spooktacular time of year.

Chocolate is undoubtedly a perennial favorite, claiming a significant share of Halloween candy sales. Chocolate bars, in particular, are the spooky stars of the season. But the world of Halloween candy extends far beyond chocolate. The variety is a testament to the diverse tastes of consumers. From tangy to tart, chewy to gummy, there is something for everyone. These candies have one thing in common: they are the finest non-chocolate candies, appreciated by confectionery enthusiasts far and wide.

The survey conducted by the National Confectioners Association revealed the favorite treats of Americans for the 2021 Halloween season. The results were clear, the love of candy remains strong. This showcases a preference, highlighting that despite the prevalence of Halloween candy, other holidays, particularly Easter, are favored for their candy selections. Notably, 57% of participants indicated that they consider Easter candy to be superior to Halloween candy.

Beyond the established favorites, the Halloween season brings an opportunity to explore the unique regional preferences for treats. Candy Store has identified Sour Patch Kids as a top choice in Alaska. Double Bubble is considered a classic in Montana, according to candystore.com, while Swedish Fish claims the win in Georgia. These regional distinctions reflect the diverse preferences of consumers across the United States, adding a layer of variety to the nationwide celebration. They also highlight that candy choices are not only personal but can be strongly influenced by location, indicating the breadth and depth of consumer preferences during the Halloween season.

The joy of Halloween extends beyond just the candy itself. Halloween decorations, both inside and out, play an essential role in setting the scene. Themed parties and gatherings are elevated by the creativity of homemade desserts and snacks.

For those seeking some inspiration for Halloween-themed treats, there is a wide array of options available. There are fun recipes for parties, movie nights, bake sales, or festive gatherings of any kind. You can create your own spooky treats, perfect for sharing with friends. There are Halloween rice krispies treats and themed party ideas that would be perfect for a Halloween potluck. From spooky ghosts to monsters, to cute cakes to cookies, you can find all the Halloween party inspiration you could ever need.

Born in Barcelona, Spain, on June 16, 1978, Daniel Brühl has always been surrounded by the arts. His German father, a businessman, and his Spanish mother, a teacher, nurtured his creative spirit from a young age. Brühl's multicultural upbringing has significantly influenced his career, allowing him to work in both Spanish and German-language films, as well as Hollywood productions.

Brühl's breakthrough came with the 2003 film "Good Bye Lenin!" which garnered critical acclaim and established him as a rising star in the film industry.

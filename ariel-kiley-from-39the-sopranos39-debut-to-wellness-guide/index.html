It’s a way of working with the physical sensations and responses that often accompany trauma, rather than just talking about the events themselves. This is, in a way, a deep and very sensitive area of work.

As an SE practitioner, Ariel Kiley guides individuals through processes that help them gently discharge the physiological residue of trauma. This might involve tracking subtle body sensations, movement, and breath, allowing the nervous system to regulate itself more effectively. It’s about helping the body complete actions that were interrupted during a traumatic experience, which, in some respects, can lead to a greater sense of calm and safety. This practice is, basically, about restoring a person’s natural resilience and capacity for self-regulation, helping them feel more settled and whole. She has, quite frankly, spent a good deal of time helping people with this very important work.

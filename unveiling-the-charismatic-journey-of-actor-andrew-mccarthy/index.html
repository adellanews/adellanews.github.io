What is the Biography of Actor Andrew McCarthy?

Andrew McCarthy was born on November 29, 1962, in Westfield, New Jersey. He grew up in a middle-class family, with his father working as a businessman and his mother as a homemaker. McCarthy attended the Dwight-Englewood School in New Jersey and later graduated from the Catholic University of America in Washington, D.C., where he studied acting.

What Are Some Iconic Roles Played by Actor Andrew McCarthy?

Actor Andrew McCarthy gained immense popularity in the 1980s, thanks to his roles in several iconic films. Some of his most memorable performances include:

  • Sixteen Candles (1984) - McCarthy played the charming and awkward Jake Ryan, who captured the heart of the film's protagonist, Samantha.
  • Pretty in Pink (1986) - As the rich and sensitive Blane McDonough, McCarthy showcased his ability to portray complex characters in teenage romance.
  • Weekend at Bernie's (1989) - In this comedic classic, McCarthy starred as Larry Wilson, who, along with his friend, tries to maintain the illusion that their deceased boss is still alive.
  • St. Elmo's Fire (1985) - McCarthy's portrayal of Kevin Dolenz, a recent college graduate, added depth to this ensemble film about friendship and young adulthood.

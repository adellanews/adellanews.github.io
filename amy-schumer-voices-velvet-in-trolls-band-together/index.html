Amy Schumer voices Velvet in Trolls Band Together

Charlotte Adams
• Jan 04, 2026 global affairs

Can a villain truly steal the show? In DreamWorks Animation's Trolls Band Together, Velvet, voiced with comedic brilliance by Amy Schumer, proves they absolutely can. Schumer's portrayal of the power-hungry pop star elevates the film beyond its vibrant, glitter-infused world, offering a nuanced exploration of ambition, sibling dynamics, and the allure of fame.

Velvet isn't just a one-dimensional antagonist. Her relentless pursuit of musical stardom, fueled by a desire to escape a perceived lackluster existence, adds depth to her villainy. Kidnapping Floyd, a former member of the boy band BroZone, and siphoning his musical talent becomes her twisted path to success. This desperate act underscores a deeper insecurity, a hunger for validation that resonates with a surprising level of relatability. While her actions are undeniably cruel, particularly towards Floyd and his brothers, Schumer's comedic timing and delivery inject a layer of dark humor that prevents Velvet from becoming purely despicable. Instead, she becomes a fascinating study in the complexities of ambition gone awry.

The introduction of Velvet and her twin brother, Veneer, adds a fresh layer of intrigue to the Trolls franchise. These new characters are not mere additions to the colorful ensemble; they represent a darker, more complex force within the typically harmonious world of the Trolls. While Velvet takes center stage as the primary antagonist, Veneers more passive role highlights the contrasting dynamics of their sibling relationship. Their shared history and differing approaches to achieving recognition add a layer of emotional depth to their story.

March 8, 2025, marks a significant date for fans of the Trolls franchise. The release of Trolls Band Together introduced audiences to Velvet and Veneer, characters that challenged the established norms of the Trolls universe. Velvet's ruthless pursuit of fame, achieved through the exploitation of Floyds musical talent, sets her apart from previous antagonists. Her actions force the protagonists to confront a new kind of threat, one that goes beyond mere differences in musical taste.

Amy Schumer's performance is crucial to the film's success. She manages to balance the villainous nature of Velvet with a comedic sensibility that keeps the character engaging and entertaining. Her vocal performance captures the nuances of Velvet's personality, from the manipulative charm she uses to ensnare Floyd to the raw desperation that drives her ambition. Schumer's comedic timing allows for moments of levity amidst the tension, creating a multi-faceted antagonist who is both menacing and strangely sympathetic. Brianna Mazzolas singing voice adds another layer to Velvet's character, giving her a powerful stage presence that belies her underhanded tactics.

The films exploration of musical talent also raises intriguing questions. Velvets belief that talent can be stolen reveals a fundamental misunderstanding of the creative process. The contrast between her manufactured pop persona and the genuine musicality of the other Trolls highlights the difference between imitation and true artistry. This theme resonates throughout the film, adding a layer of commentary on the nature of creativity and the pitfalls of seeking fame at any cost. The vibrant animation, catchy musical numbers, and talented voice cast, including Anna Kendrick reprising her role as Poppy, create a visually stunning and aurally engaging experience. But it's Amy Schumer's portrayal of Velvet that truly captivates, leaving a lasting impression long after the credits roll. She doesn't just steal talent; she steals the show.

The Korean dub of Trolls World Tour, released on April 29, 2020, further expands the reach of the franchise. This international release demonstrates the global appeal of the Trolls universe and its ability to transcend cultural barriers. While the specific actors involved in the Korean dub may not be as widely recognized in Western audiences, their contribution is vital to bringing the story to a wider audience. The fact that the film was released simultaneously in theaters and on VOD platforms speaks to the changing landscape of film distribution in the digital age.

The success of Trolls Band Together lies in its ability to blend vibrant visuals, catchy music, and compelling characters. Velvet's villainous arc, fueled by Schumer's comedic genius, adds a layer of complexity that elevates the film beyond a simple children's movie. It explores themes of ambition, sibling rivalry, and the true meaning of talent, offering a nuanced and entertaining experience for audiences of all ages.

Who is Pete Sampras? A Brief Biography

Pete Sampras is a former professional tennis player from the United States, widely regarded as one of the greatest players in the history of the sport. Born on August 12, 1971, in Potomac, Maryland, Sampras began playing tennis at a young age and quickly rose through the ranks. He turned professional in 1988 and enjoyed a stellar career that lasted until 2002. With 14 Grand Slam singles titles, Sampras held the record for the most major titles won by a male player until it was surpassed by Roger Federer in 2009.
